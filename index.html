na hora de pôr a mesa, éramos cinco

Aug 23, 2024

by José Luís Peixoto

when it was time to set the table, we were five:
my mom, my dad, my sisters,
and me. then, my older sister
got married. then, my younger sister
got married. then, my dad died. today,
when it's time to set the table, we are five,
besides my older sister who is
at her house, besides my younger
sister who is at her house, besides my
father, besides my widowed mother. each of them
is an empty seat at this table where
I eat alone. but they will always be here.
when it's time to set the table, we will always be five.
as long as one of us is alive, we will
always be five.

Amar

Aug 24, 2024

by Florbela Espanca

I want to love, love deeply and unbound!
To love for love's own sake, both near and far,
To love this one, that one, all around...
To love, to love! And not a single heart!

Remember? Forget? It matters not at all!
To hold or to release—who's to say?
Who claims a love can last through rise and fall
For all of life is surely led astray!

There's a Spring in every life we live:
We must sing it forth in bloom so bright,
For if God gave us voice, it was to give!

And if one day I turn to dust and end,
Let my night be dawn, a light to send,
To lose myself... and find my way aright...
