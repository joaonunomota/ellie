Transforma-se o amador na cousa amada

Aug 23, 2024

by Luís de Camões

The lover turns into the thing beloved,
By virtue of much imagining;
So I have nothing left to wish or cling,
For in myself I have my wish uncovered.

If in her my soul has thus been melded,
What more can this body hope to bring?
In itself alone it rests, a simple thing,
To such a soul so perfectly connected.

But this pure and beautiful half-divine idea,
Like an accident within its place,
Conforms itself with my soul's inner norm.

It dwells in thought, as the ideal near;
And the pure love from which I draw my grace,
Seeks, like simple matter, its true form.

Amar

Aug 24, 2024

by Florbela Espanca

I want to love, love deeply and unbound!
To love for love's own sake, both near and far,
To love this one, that one, all around...
To love, to love! And not a single heart!

Remember? Forget? It matters not at all!
To hold or to release—who's to say?
Who claims a love can last through rise and fall
For all of life is surely led astray!

There's a Spring in every life we live:
We must sing it forth in bloom so bright,
For if God gave us voice, it was to give!

And if one day I turn to dust and end,
Let my night be dawn, a light to send,
To lose myself... and find my way aright...
